Common Cyber Threats You Should Know

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity through emails, messages, or fake websites.

Learn More

Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts your files and demands payment to restore access, often spreading through phishing emails or security vulnerabilities.

Learn More

Identity Theft

The fraudulent acquisition and use of a person's private identifying information, usually for financial gain or to gain access to personal accounts.

Essential Security Practices

Strong Passwords

Use unique, complex passwords for each account and consider a password manager to keep track of them securely.

Two-Factor Auth

Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible to add an extra layer of security beyond just passwords.

Regular Updates

Keep your devices, software, and apps updated with the latest security patches and improvements.

Stay Vigilant

Be cautious about clicking links, opening attachments, or providing personal information, especially from unknown sources.
